NO. 5/NO. 24

MARK ROTHKO, 1948

Two numbers. No name. No. 5. Or No. 24. It's both. That barely counts as a title. It sounds like something written on the side of a crate. Right around now, Rothko starts dropping the titles. The idea was simple. Give a painting a name and the name gets there before you do. Call it Sunset, everybody finds the sunset, and the looking is basically over. Call it No. 5, and you're on your own. Just you and the thing. He'd already stopped painting people. Now the names start disappearing too. Two ways of getting out of your way. So look at what's here, with nothing to call it. A brown band across the middle. Black shapes, like something got burned. A pale field holding it all up. And orange pushing in around the edges. Now, one fact about 1948, said plain and then left alone. That October, his mother died. He never drew a line between that and the paintings. So neither should we. But this is the kind of thing he was making that year. No name. No figure. Nothing telling you what to feel. Just something dark, held real still.
