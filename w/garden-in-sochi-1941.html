GARDEN IN SOCHI

ARSHILE GORKY, 1941

So, your brain is going to try to sort these shapes. Bird. Face. Boot. Don't bother. Nothing in here stays put, and that's the point. Here's the strange part. Gorky painted this garden more than once. And the name is a fake-out. Because the garden wasn't in Sochi. Sochi is a Russian city on the Black Sea. Gorky never went there. The real garden was his father's, in an Armenian village called Khorkom, where he was a kid. So. Wrong place. Painted over and over. Why? When Gorky was a teenager, the Armenian Genocide tears through everything. A few years later, his mother starves to death. He said, in his arms. And the garden goes with her. Not knocked-down gone. Gone into him. Then he gets to America and builds a whole new man. He was born Vosdanig Adoian. He becomes Arshile Gorky. Tells people he's Russian. Says he's related to Maxim Gorky, the famous writer. Except Maxim Gorky was already a pen name. So he's claiming family with a name that was made up in the first place. Gorky means bitter, by the way. That part feels pretty honest. You see the pattern. He hides himself behind a Russian name. Then he takes the realest place he has, his father's garden, and hides that behind a Russian place. But the truth leaks. When MoMA bought this painting and asked about it, he didn't describe Sochi. He described home. Old apple trees. Wild carrots. Porcupines nesting in the shade. A blue rock half buried in black earth, where the village women rubbed their breasts against the stone to make their wishes come true. A holy tree covered in strips of cloth torn from people's clothes. Khorkom. Not Sochi. Now look again. That big dark blue shape holding down the middle. Those little red and yellow strips hanging on the left. He never labels anything. But come on. And the shapes? Now you know why they won't sit still. This was never a place. It's a memory. And memories move. There was nothing left to visit. No village. No garden. No her. Except this. So he kept coming back. Not because he solved it. Because he couldn't.
